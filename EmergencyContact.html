First Aid Assistance

Bleeding and Wounds

Control bleeding by applying direct pressure with a clean cloth or bandage. Elevate the injured area if possible. Clean minor wounds with running water and cover with a sterile bandage.

Burns and Scalds

For minor burns, cool the area under running water for 10-20 minutes. Do not apply creams or ointments. For severe burns, cover with a sterile non-stick dressing and seek immediate medical attention.

Choking

If the person cannot cough or breathe, perform abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver) or give 5 firm back blows. Call emergency services if the obstruction is not removed.

Heart Attack

If you suspect a heart attack, call 193 or 112 immediately. Keep the person calm and seated. Loosen tight clothing. If trained, administer CPR if they become unresponsive.

Snake Bites

Keep the person calm and immobilize the bitten area below heart level. Do not attempt to suck out venom or apply ice. Seek immediate medical attention.

Heatstroke

Move the person to a cool, shaded area. Apply cool, damp cloths or immerse them in cool water. Give small sips of water if they are conscious. Call emergency services if symptoms worsen.

Poisoning

Call 112 or 193 immediately. Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by a medical professional. Try to identify the poison and provide this information to emergency responders.
